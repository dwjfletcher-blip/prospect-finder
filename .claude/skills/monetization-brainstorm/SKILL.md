# Skill: Monetization Brainstorm

Evaluate a revenue idea using structured business logic and Hormozi-informed frameworks.

## When to Use

When David has a business or revenue idea and wants to stress-test it, compare it against alternatives, or decide if it's worth pursuing.

## How to Run This Skill

1. Ask for the idea. One sentence is enough to start.
2. Ask the clarifying questions below — only what's missing.
3. Run the idea through the evaluation framework.
4. Give a clear recommendation at the end: pursue, park, or kill — with reasoning.

## Clarifying Questions

- Who specifically pays for this? (Don't accept "businesses" — push for a specific type)
- What problem does it solve, and how painful is that problem on a scale of 1-10?
- Is this a one-time transaction, recurring, or usage-based?
- What does David have to do to deliver it? (Time, skills, tools)
- Are there already people paying for something like this?

## Evaluation Framework

Score each dimension 1-5. Be honest — a 3 is not a compliment.

**1. Dream Outcome (Hormozi: $100M Offers)**
Does the buyer have a vivid, desirable result they're paying for — or are they buying a vague service?
- 5: Clear, specific outcome ("my business gets 10 new leads/month")
- 1: Vague deliverable ("better website")

**2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement**
Does the buyer believe David can actually deliver? Is there proof, a portfolio, or a guarantee that reduces risk?
- 5: Strong credibility signals or risk reversal in place
- 1: No proof, no guarantee, buyer has to trust blindly

**3. Time to Value**
How quickly does the buyer feel the result?
- 5: Immediate or within days
- 1: Months before any visible result

**4. Effort & Sacrifice**
What does the buyer have to do to get the result? Lower friction = higher value.
- 5: David handles everything, client does almost nothing
- 1: Client has to do significant work themselves

**5. Scalability**
Can David do more of this without proportionally more time?
- 5: Productized, templated, or automated — scales well
- 1: Pure hourly trade — hits a ceiling fast

**6. Lead Generation Fit (Hormozi: $100M Leads)**
Is there a clear, repeatable way to find and reach buyers for this?
- 5: Easy to identify and reach (e.g., Lead Scout finds them directly)
- 1: No obvious acquisition channel

**7. Financial Model Fit (Hormozi: Money Models)**
Does the revenue model work for David's current stage?
- 5: Fast cash, low overhead, good margin
- 1: Long sales cycle, high overhead, or low margin

**If Pursuing — Immediate Next Step:** [The single most important action to take right now]

**Variants to Consider:** [1-3 tweaks to the model that could improve the score — only if worth exploring]

---

## Hard Rules

- Never give a "pursue" verdict on a score below 20/35 without a clear path to fixing the weak dimensions.
- Always identify the single biggest risk before recommending pursuit.
- David is early-stage and cash-constrained — weight Time to Value and Financial Model Fit heavily.
- Don't generate 10 ideas. Evaluate the one idea deeply. Volume of ideas is not the goal.
