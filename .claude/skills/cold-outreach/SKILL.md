# Skill: Cold Outreach

Draft a concise, confident cold email to a prospective business client.

## When to Use

When David has identified a lead (via Lead Scout or otherwise) and needs to reach out cold — no prior relationship, no referral.

## How to Run This Skill

1. Ask for the inputs below. Ask all at once.
2. Generate the email using the principles and format below.
3. Always produce two subject line options.

## Inputs Needed

- Business name and type (e.g., "Mario's Pizza, a local restaurant")
- Specific problem identified (no website, bad website, slow site, outdated design, etc.)
- Any personalization detail (something specific about their business — name of owner if known, something visible on their current site or Google listing)
- Desired outcome of the email (book a call, get a reply, send portfolio)

## Writing Principles

**The goal is one thing: get a reply.** Not to sell. Not to explain everything. Just start a conversation.

- Lead with their problem, not David's services. They don't care what David does — they care about their own business.
- Be specific. Generic compliments ("I noticed your business") get deleted. Name something real.
- Keep it under 100 words in the body. Shorter is better. Busy business owners don't read essays.
- One clear ask at the end. Not multiple options — one action.
- No attachments on the first email. Portfolio link is fine if it fits naturally.
- Tone: direct, respectful, confident. Not salesy. Not groveling.
- Never say "I hope this email finds you well." Never.

## Output Format

---

**Subject Option 1:** [Direct, specific to their problem]
**Subject Option 2:** [Curiosity-based, shorter]

---

**Email Body:**

Hi [Name / "there" if unknown],

[1-2 sentences: specific observation about their current web presence — what's missing or broken. No fluff.]

[1 sentence: what David does and who he does it for.]

[1 sentence: the specific outcome they'd get.]

[Single CTA: "Worth a quick call this week?" or "I can send over a few examples if you'd like."]

— David Fletcher
[Portfolio URL once it exists]

---

**Notes for David (strip before sending)**
- [Personalization flags — anything that could be made more specific]
- [Any tone or length warnings]

---

## Hard Rules

- Never lead with "My name is David and I..." — they don't care yet.
- Never use the word "solutions."
- Never attach anything to a cold email.
- Never send without at least one specific detail about their business.
- If David doesn't have a portfolio URL yet, omit the link entirely — don't link to something unfinished.
